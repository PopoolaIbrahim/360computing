Soil and Nutrient Management

Integrated nutrient management is the management of both organic and inorganic plant nutrients for optimal rice production while conserving the natural resource base essential for long-term sustainability. It consists of: Application of plant nutrient on the basis of soil test recommendation.

 

Water Management

Water is the most limiting factor in rice production. With adequate water supply, rice can be grown in most parts of the country. Water has a dual function of meeting physiological demands of the rice crop as well as controlling weeds. Rice requires an annual rainfall of 1200-1600mm that is evenly distributed during the growing season.

Hand weeding:

Rice fields should be weeded regularly, especially during the early stages of growth. First weeding should be thorough and should be done within 2 to 3 weeks after emergence, using hoes. (The earlier the first weeding is done the better).
Second weeding should be done 5 to 6 weeks after emergence. Weed a third time, if necessary.

Use of herbicides:

Pre-emergence:

Oxidiazon at 3.0kg a.i. ha-1 (4-5 liters Ronstar 25 EC/ha)
Roundup (3 litres ha) applied pre- land preparation, Or spray 3litres of Gramoxone applied pre-emergence (1-3 days after planting) along with 4-5 litres of Oxidiaxone or 5-6 litresButachlor 50 or 60EC as tank mixture.


Post-emergence:

Propanil + bentazon at 3.0kg a.i. ha-1( 5litresBasagram 500PLha-1)
Propanil + Fluorodifen at 3.0kg a.i. ha-1 (5 litresRisane 3000ECha-1)
Propanil + thiobencarb at 3.0kg a.i. ha-1( 5litresTamarice 336ECha-1) 2 - 3 weeks after planting
Propanil + 2.4D Amine at 3.0kg ai ha-1
